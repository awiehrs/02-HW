Digital & Analog Photography

Blending mediums of artwork together has been a passion of mine for over 15 years. I began by making collages in high school from drawings, magazine clippings, and traditional film photographs. Later, I attended Virginia Intermont College in Bristol, VA, where I learned the craft of analog photography. As I explored my own artistic styling, I began to realize my themes consistently involved natural conservation.
I hope to begin work in web development to utilize my skills in creative solutions to help others.

Grayson Highlands State Park in Virginia is home to a small herd of wild ponies. Their herd is dwindling, as many protections for wild equines are rescinded across North America.

Large format analog cameras allowed me to capture the stunning detail of frozen waterways in Appalachia. The preservation of these mountain waterways is crucial to the health of our oceans.
